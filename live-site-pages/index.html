Who We Are

A Unique Public-Private Partnership

PFC Associates, a joint venture of Providence Hospital and the Washington Hospital Center, initiated a unique privatization project with the government of the District of Columbia to deliver occupational medicine and behavioral health services to the city's first responders.

★

Partnerships that improve the quality of medical care for police officers and firefighters.

Workforce Productivity

Boosting public safety workforce health and operational readiness.

✓

Cost Savings

Generating significant cost savings for the District of Columbia.

Our Mission
PFC Associates, LLC, will develop and practice innovative approaches to appraising, initiating, maintaining, and restoring the physical, psychological and emotional health of Police Officers, Firefighters and other members covered in the District of Columbia whom we serve.

What We Do

Comprehensive Medical Services

From routine fitness-for-duty exams to behavioral health counseling, we offer a full spectrum of occupational medicine for D.C.'s first responders.

📋

Physical Examinations

Annual and biennial Fitness for Duty Exams, Pre-Employment, Return to Duty, and Retirement examinations.

⚡

Injury Management

Acute work-related injury treatment, short-term disability management, and duty status assessments.

💬

Behavioral Health

Counseling for occupational illness, applicant screening, fitness evaluations, and post-critical incident assessments.

🧪

COVID-19 Testing

On-site testing conducted at MPD or FEMS Medical Services request with advance registration.

💓

Stress Tests

Cardiac function evaluation during physical activity using treadmill or stationary bike with vital monitoring.

🌬

Spirometry Testing

NIOSH-approved pulmonary function measurement of air volume and flow capacity.

⚔

Wellness Services

Immunizations, nutritional guidance, smoking cessation, TB screening, flu shots, cancer screening, and stress reduction.

📄

Case Management

Coordination of care for timely, efficient treatment with designated RN Case Managers.

🛡

Bloodborne Pathogen

Post-exposure surveillance and treatment services for occupational exposures.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about clinic hours, appointments, and patient services.

The Police and Fire Clinic is open 0700 – 2300 hours, Monday through Friday. The clinic is closed during major holidays.
When the clinic is closed, you may visit:

Emergency Rooms:
• MedStar Washington Hospital Center
• MedStar Georgetown University Hospital Center

MedStar Prompt Care:
• Adams Morgan: 1805 Columbia Road N.W. — (202) 797-4960, 8AM–8PM daily
• Capitol Hill: 228 7th Street S.E. — (202) 698-0795, 8AM–8PM daily

Providence Urgent Care Center:
• 11500 Varnum Street N.E. — (202) 854-7001, Mon–Fri 8AM–10PM; Sat–Sun 8AM–3PM
Contact the National Suicide Prevention Hotline at 1-800-273-8255 for 24/7 trained counselors. You can also dial 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline).
PFC does not cover expenses for outside specialists without a referral. Private insurance may deny reimbursement due to workers' compensation classification.
Wear loose-fitting sweats for lower body injuries, sleeveless shirts for shoulder issues, and supportive shoes — never flip-flops.
You may wear regular clothing, but no jewelry, hair pins, or metal objects. If sedation is required, you must arrange transportation in advance.
Complete a release form including your name, date of birth, service dates, and signature. Processing takes 3–5 business days.
Contact your liaison or your PFC provider directly to reschedule.
Bring a photo ID, any relevant medical records, and imaging related to your injury or condition.
PFC provides COVID-19 testing for members every Monday, Wednesday, and Friday starting at 9:30 AM with advance registration required.
